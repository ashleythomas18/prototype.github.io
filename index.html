Coal production is a major industry in several countries, with China, India, and the United States leading the global output. India is the second-largest coal producer, accounting for a significant portion of its energy needs. The country primarily relies on coal for electricity generation, with major coalfields located in states like Jharkhand, Chhattisgarh, Odisha, and West Bengal. India's coal production is vital to its industrial sector, but it also poses environmental challenges due to carbon emissions. As the country strives to balance economic growth and environmental sustainability, efforts to improve mining efficiency and reduce emissions are crucial.
